01
The problem
Introduction

Customer churn - the rate at which customers cease doing business with a company over time - is one of the most critical metrics in e-commerce. In a fiercely competitive landscape, customers switch platforms with minimal friction: better pricing, preferred payment methods, or a single poor service experience can drive them away permanently.

Data science enables churn prediction by analysing customer interaction patterns and training machine learning models to identify at-risk customers before they leave - enabling proactive, cost-effective retention rather than expensive re-acquisition.

02
Real-world obstacles
Challenges in Data Acquisition

Building a reliable churn prediction model starts with obtaining quality data - a process riddled with real-world obstacles. Integrating data from CRM, billing, shipping, customer service, and marketing systems introduces consistency issues. Class imbalance - where churners are a small minority - introduces bias that must be addressed through resampling or cost-sensitive learning.

03
Data preparation
Pre-Processing & Feature Engineering

The preprocessing pipeline was guided by five core principles. Each step ensures the dataset is clean, consistent, and model-ready before any algorithm is applied.

# Principle What it means
01 Validity Values conform to defined domain rules and formats (e.g. no negative ages, valid date ranges)
02 Accuracy Incorrect or misleading values are corrected or removed to ensure true representation
03 Completeness Missing values are imputed using mean/median or flagged - no column left unaddressed
04 Consistency Conflicting values across joined datasets resolved - e.g. duplicate customer records reconciled
05 Uniformity Units, date formats, and categorical encodings standardised across all data sources

When predictor variables are highly correlated, isolating their individual effects becomes unreliable. Two feature selection approaches were evaluated to handle multicollinearity before model training.

Criteria Backward Elimination Principal Component Analysis (PCA)
Original features preserved ✓ Yes - retains interpretable predictors ✗ No - transforms into components
Interpretability ✓ High - coefficients remain meaningful ✗ Low - components lack direct meaning
Handles multicollinearity ✓ Removes correlated predictors ✓ Eliminates via orthogonal transformation
Dimensionality reduction ✗ Limited - only removes insignificant vars ✓ Strong - compresses many features into fewer components
Computational cost ✓ Low - iterative p-value checks ✗ Higher - requires eigenvalue decomposition
Best suited for ✓ Small-to-mid feature sets needing transparency ✓ High-dimensional data with heavy correlation

Algorithm evaluation
Selecting the Right Classifier

No single model is universally superior. The right choice depends on the business context - whether the priority is minimising false positives (unnecessary retention spend) or maximising true positives (catching every at-risk customer).

Logistic Regression

Interpretable baseline - clear probability outputs for each customer

Decision Trees

Explainable rules for stakeholders - easy to visualise and communicate

Random Forests

Robust ensemble with feature importance - reduces overfitting through averaging

AdaBoost

Adapts to misclassified samples - iteratively focuses on harder-to-predict cases

Stacking Classifier

Combines multiple models for improved generalisation across diverse patterns

Support Vector Machines

Effective in high-dimensional spaces - strong boundary separation for complex data

Confusion matrix - TP, TN, FP, FN with derived evaluation formulas

Accuracy

(TP + TN) / (TP + TN + FP + FN)

Frequency of correct predictions. Most reliable when classes are balanced.

Precision

TP / (TP + FP)

Accuracy of positive predictions. High when cost of false alarms is critical.

Sensitivity (Recall)

TP / (TP + FN)

Actual churners correctly identified. High recall = fewer customers lost.

Specificity

TN / (TN + FP)

Correct non-churner identification - prevents wasted retention spend.

F1-Score

2 × (Precision × Recall) / (Precision + Recall)

Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Ideal for imbalanced datasets.

ROC AUC

Area under the ROC curve

Model's ability to distinguish outcomes across all classification thresholds.

05
Understanding who churns
Customer Segmentation

Effective churn prevention requires understanding who is churning and why. Dividing customers into groups with shared characteristics enables targeted, personalised retention strategies.

Geographic

Regional preferences, delivery optimisation, seasonal variations, and cultural sensitivity to tailor offers and logistics by location.

Demographic

Age, income, and location-based targeting to refine pricing strategies, communication channels, and brand loyalty programmes.

Psychographic

Purchase patterns, engagement levels, and journey mapping to drive cross-selling, loyalty rewards, and churn prediction.

Behavioral

Lifestyle preferences, purchase motivations, and emotional triggers to differentiate service and enhance the customer experience.

06
Turning insight into action
Business Recommendations

Accurate predictions are only valuable when paired with actionable strategies. Ten key recommendations derived from this analysis:

Leverage loyal customer insights

Analyse loyal customer profiles to identify which incentives resonate - then apply those patterns to at-risk segments.

Offer targeted incentives

Provide rewards, loyalty points, and limited-time coupons targeted at high-risk and long-term customers.

Enhance delivery & payment options

Improve fulfilment speed and expand payment methods to reduce friction at key conversion points.

Form strategic partnerships

Collaborate with banks and financial partners for exclusive discounts on card-based purchases.

Provide excellent customer service

Ensure fast, efficient issue resolution - poor support is one of the top churn drivers.

Enhance the mobile experience

Build mobile-first platforms with push notification capabilities to maintain engagement across channels.

Drive meaningful engagement

Use email, social media, and in-app notifications to share personalised product and promotion content.

Collect and act on feedback

Systematically gather feedback and feed it into product and service improvement cycles.

Continuously monitor and adapt

Refine models and segmentation as customer behaviour evolves - static models degrade over time.

Address pain points proactively

Use the model's risk scores to trigger outreach before customers decide to leave, not after.

Business Impact
Retain beats re-acquire
Proactive ML-driven retention is significantly more cost-effective than re-acquisition - early scoring enables targeted outreach before customers decide to leave.
Model Finding
Ensemble methods to cover the loss
Random Forest and Stacking Classifier consistently outperform single classifiers - delivering the strongest precision-recall balance for churn detection.
Value creation
Proactiveness rewards in long-term

Customer acquisition requires substantial investment - but sustainable growth is built on retention. Churn prediction enables proactive intervention, allowing businesses to redirect resources toward high-risk customers with personalised, timely outreach. Continuous refinement of models based on evolving customer behaviour ensures sustained competitive advantage in an increasingly data-driven industry.
