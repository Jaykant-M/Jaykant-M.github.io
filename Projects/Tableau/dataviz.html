01
Background
Purpose & Inspiration

This project was driven by a belief in education's transformative power. Despite rising school enrollment globally, UNICEF data reveals that 600 million children and adolescents worldwide are unable to attain minimum proficiency in reading and mathematics.

Growing up in India gave me a first-hand perspective on how poverty, sanitation, and water access silently shape learning outcomes — factors that rarely appear in traditional academic reporting but are deeply correlated with educational performance.

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." — Nelson Mandela

Framing the challenge — education access as a global development goal

02
Interactive Visualisation
Live Dashboard

UNICEF: Education & Beyond — Interactive Dashboard

Dashboard architecture — filters, parameters, and calculated fields

The dashboard provides interactive filters, dynamic charts, and custom visualizations letting users explore:

  • School enrollment rates across countries and regions
  • Education expenditure as a percentage of GDP
  • Gender disparities in literacy and enrollment
  • Child labor rates and their correlation with dropout patterns
  • Infrastructure indicators: water access, sanitation, electricity
03
Research foundation
Data Sources

To ensure credibility and breadth of coverage, data was sourced from:

  • UNESCO — Global education statistics and learning outcomes
  • World Bank — Economic indicators and infrastructure data
  • National education ministries — Country-level enrollment and expenditure figures

All datasets were cleaned and joined in Tableau Prep before visualization.

04
What the data revealed
Key Insights

"Schools lacking drinking water show measurably lower attendance rates — infrastructure and education are inseparable."

Key finding from the dashboard analysis
  • Significant urban-rural disparities in educational access persist across developing regions
  • Out-of-school rates increase sharply between lower primary and secondary levels
  • Schools lacking drinking water show measurably lower attendance rates
  • Poverty directly affects both the affordability and quality of education
  • Gender literacy gaps signal structural barriers to equal education access
05
Design decisions
User Experience

The dashboard was designed for diverse stakeholders — policymakers, educators, and researchers — regardless of technical expertise. Navigation is intuitive, with clear labelling and consistent color coding to distinguish regions and categories.

Custom tooltips surface contextual information without cluttering the visual space, keeping the focus on the story the data is telling.

Metric selection — balancing depth with clarity

06
Looking ahead
Impact & Future Directions

The dashboard was built to inform strategic initiatives around global education access. Potential expansions include:

  • Adding health and nutrition datasets to enrich the correlation analysis
  • Integrating time-series views to track progress across decades
  • Building a country-level drill-through for deeper local insights

Technical Skills

  • Tableau Desktop — calculated fields, LOD expressions, parameter actions
  • Data cleaning and reshaping in Tableau Prep
  • Cross-dataset joins and blending
  • Statistical analysis and insight interpretation

Challenges & Learnings

Integrating multiple datasets with inconsistent country naming conventions required careful data wrangling. Balancing visual complexity with clarity taught me to ruthlessly prioritize the most impactful metrics and progressively reveal detail on demand.
