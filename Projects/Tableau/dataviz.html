01
European Student Data Viz Challenge 2023
The Challenge

In 2023, while studying at EDHEC Business School's Lille campus, I entered the European Student Data Viz Challenge - organised by EDHEC in partnership with UNICEF, Tableau, and Mazars, drawing entries from students at 16 top European business schools and universities across 9 countries, including INSEAD, ESMT, the London School of Economics, Dublin City University, and Católica Lisbon. The dashboard was selected as one of 10 finalists, which meant travelling to Paris to pitch it live in front of a judging panel drawn from all four partner organisations, at EDHEC's Paris Opera campus on 12 May 2023.

02
Background
Purpose & Inspiration

This project was driven by a belief in education's transformative power. Despite rising school enrollment globally, UNICEF data reveals that 600 million children and adolescents worldwide are unable to attain minimum proficiency in reading and mathematics.

Growing up in India gave me a first-hand perspective on how poverty, sanitation, and water access silently shape learning outcomes - factors that rarely appear in traditional academic reporting but are deeply correlated with educational performance.

03
Interactive Visualisation
Live Dashboard

UNICEF: Education & Beyond - Interactive Dashboard

The dashboard is built around three connected lenses - access, infrastructure, and poverty - rather than a single chart. Interactive filters, dynamic charts, and custom visualizations let users explore:

  • School enrollment rates across countries and regions
  • Education expenditure as a percentage of GDP
  • Gender disparities in literacy and enrollment
  • Child labor rates and their correlation with dropout patterns
  • Infrastructure indicators: water access, sanitation, electricity
04
Research foundation
Data Sources

UNESCO

Global education statistics and learning outcomes across countries and regions.

World Bank

Economic indicators and infrastructure data including GDP expenditure on education.

National Ministries

Country-level enrollment and expenditure figures from education ministries.

All datasets were cleaned and joined in Tableau Prep before visualization.

05
What the data revealed
Key Insights

Urban-Rural Gap

Significant disparities in educational access persist between urban and rural populations across developing regions.

Water & Attendance

1 in 5 children attend a school with no basic drinking water service - schools lacking it show measurably lower attendance (WHO/UNICEF).

Secondary Dropout

Out-of-school rates increase sharply between lower primary and secondary levels, signalling a critical transition gap.

Poverty Exclusion

Children from the poorest 20% of households are 8× more likely to be out of school than those from the richest 20% (UNESCO GEM Report).

Gender Literacy Gap

Gender literacy gaps signal structural barriers to equal education access, particularly in South Asia and Sub-Saharan Africa.

06
Design decisions
User Experience

The dashboard was designed for diverse stakeholders - policymakers, educators, and researchers - regardless of technical expertise. Navigation is intuitive, with clear labelling and consistent color coding to distinguish regions and categories. Custom tooltips surface contextual information without cluttering the visual space, keeping the focus on the story the data is telling.

07
Looking ahead
Impact & Future Directions

The dashboard was built to inform strategic initiatives around global education access. Potential expansions include:

  • Adding health and nutrition datasets to enrich the correlation analysis
  • Integrating time-series views to track progress across decades
  • Building a country-level drill-through for deeper local insights

Technical Skills

  • Tableau Desktop - calculated fields, LOD expressions, parameter actions
  • Data cleaning and reshaping in Tableau Prep
  • Cross-dataset joins and blending
  • Statistical analysis and insight interpretation

Challenges & Learnings

Integrating multiple datasets with inconsistencies required careful data wrangling. Balancing visual complexity with clarity taught me to ruthlessly prioritize the most impactful metrics and progressively reveal detail on demand.

The harder challenge, though, was translating a data finding into a live pitch for a mixed judging panel - getting from "I found a correlation between water access and attendance" to something a room could feel, not just read off a chart.
